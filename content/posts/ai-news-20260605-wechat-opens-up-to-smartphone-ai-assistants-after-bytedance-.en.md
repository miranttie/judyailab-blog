## 📰 Key Takeaways

> Tencent has quietly opened deep WeChat integration to several smartphone makers, allowing some users of Huawei, Honor, Xiaomi, OPPO, and vivo to control WeChat features via their built-in voice assistants using natural language commands, including sending messages and initiating voice or video calls. This marks the first time WeChat has significantly loosened restrictions on AI phone integration, against the backdrop of market pushback after ByteDance launched its AI phone, prompting the industry to reconsider the trade-off between open and closed strategies. Meanwhile, Tencent is also exploring AI payment features in its enterprise AI agent "WorkBuddy," researching the feasibility of completing financial transactions within the agent execution framework. However, this deep integration of AI phones with communication and payment apps has already triggered concerns and resistance from financial institutions, with main worries focused on compliance risks and security vulnerabilities after AI assistants介入支付及即時通訊流程後的合規風險與資安漏洞。目前各品牌的接入範圍仍限定於特定用戶，屬於有限度測試階段，尚未全面對外開放。 Currently, each brand's access is still limited to specific users, in a limited testing phase, and has not yet been fully opened to the public.

## 💬 JudyAI Lab Perspective

Tencent opening deep WeChat integration to multiple phone makers is the first time a super-app has significantly loosened restrictions on AI integration. We believe this signal goes far beyond just a feature upgrade.

This case reveals the core trade-off platform products face when navigating the AI integration wave: ByteDance's closed AI phone launch backfired in the market, forcing the industry to reassess the balance between "full integration" and "preserving user choice." Tencent chose to loosen restrictions at the interface layer—letting voice assistants operate WeChat features—but that doesn't necessarily mean giving up core data control. This is a typical gradual opening strategy. Worth tracking alongside this is WorkBuddy's AI payment exploration: when agents can complete financial transactions within the execution framework, technical feasibility is no longer the main hurdle—it's the compliance framework lagging behind real-world deployment and financial institutions' concerns that are the real bottlenecks.

If you're designing any AI agent features involving communication or payments, move compliance assessment to the early design phase—facing regulatory barriers right before launch will cost you much more.
