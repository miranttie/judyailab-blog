## 💬 JudyAI Lab's View

Nvidia entering the consumer CPU market via AI Agent PCs isn't just about expanding hardware territory—it's a battle over where AI computation happens. And the stakes go way beyond that $200B market.

For years, AI inference has been tied to cloud data centers, but Nvidia's play is to let AI agents handle complex tasks locally, turning PCs from passive tools into autonomous assistants. The partnerships with Microsoft, Dell, and HP show this isn't just about selling chips—it's about connecting software, hardware, and the entire ecosystem. Here's what we're seeing: once local computing power becomes affordable, AI app design will hit a fundamental fork in the road—which scenarios are worth moving off the cloud, and which must stay there? That answer will change fast.

My advice: start listing your product features now and figure out which ones would actually deliver a better user experience if run locally. Thinking about this early beats scrambling after the hardware goes mainstream.
