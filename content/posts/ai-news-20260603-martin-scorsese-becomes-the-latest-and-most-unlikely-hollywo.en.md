## 💬 JudyAI Lab Perspective

The fact that Martin Scorsese is using AI for storyboard creation—that "localized adoption" signal—is more worth watching than any technical detail.

The point he chose to start with is thought-provoking: storyboarding is a relatively low-risk visualization step in the creative workflow, doesn't directly determine the final product quality. We've observed that this "starting from auxiliary visualization" rather than "replacing creative decisions" model is exactly how AI tools are currently being accepted by traditional creators. When a director who's long resisted the tech wave is willing to let AI into his workflow—even just for storyboard sketches—it shows that the tool's positioning and boundaries matter more than capability itself. Users don't just care about "what AI can do," but also "where AI gets involved and where it doesn't."

If you're developing or choosing AI tools, ask yourself: can it solve steps in the process that are "time-consuming but low in creative content"? Starting from there is often easier to open the first door than going straight for core creative work.
