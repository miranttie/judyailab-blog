## Midjourney killed the free trial, and it's more complicated than it seems

Midjourney went fully paid. Every plan is subscription-based—monthly or yearly with auto-renewal by default. The cheapest option starts at Basic Plan, then Standard, Pro, and Mega—the four tiers [Source: https://docs.midjourney.com/hc/en-us/articles/30246098951821-Comparing-Midjourney-Plans].

For design studios that already use Midjourney as their main tool, this is no big deal. The monthly fee is just part of doing business, same as paying for Adobe.

But for solopreneurs, it's different. Their subscription list probably already has Notion, Buttondown, ChatGPT, Canva, domains, hosting… adding one more monthly fee hurts. The question isn't "Is Midjourney worth it?" but "Do I really want to sign up for another card just to generate three images a week?"

Looking at recent discussions on Reddit r/midjourney, Indie Hackers, and several AI tool review sites, a lot of indie creators are stuck on the same issue—it's not that they don't want to pay, they just don't want to lock into another subscription for sporadic marketing needs.

## Why Flux, Ideogram, and SeaArt—and why everything else got cut

There are plenty of Midjourney alternatives out there—from DALL-E 3, Adobe Firefly, to some Chinese AI image platforms. Here's why they didn't make the cut:

DALL-E 3 is bundled inside ChatGPT Plus, which means you're still paying a monthly fee, so it doesn't solve the same pain point as Midjourney [Source: https://openai.com/index/dall-e-3/]. Adobe Firefly's interface is clunky for non-designers, and the learning curve isn't worth it [Source: https://www.adobe.com/products/firefly.html]. Those Chinese-owned tools that market themselves as "Chinese prompt friendly"? The outputs tend to feel generic and canned—a dead giveaway when used for marketing assets.

So here's why these three made the cut:

**Flux**—an open-source image generation model from Black Forest Labs, can run free or cheap on multiple platforms (Replicate, Together AI, fal.ai, etc.). The community sees it as currently the closest open-source option to Midjourney v6 quality [Source: https://blackforestlabs.ai/announcing-black-forest-labs/].

**Ideogram**—its text rendering is the standout feature. For scenarios like logos, posters, and sales page heroes where you need English text stably placed in the image, public benchmarks consistently rank it #1. The free version has daily credits [Source: https://about.ideogram.ai/2.0].

**SeaArt**—offers free credits daily, with an interface similar to a stripped-down Civitai. They have a huge library of community models, and Chinese prompt适配度 gets relatively stable reviews in Mandarin-speaking communities [Source: https://www.seaart.ai/].

## Five scenarios broken down—differences are more obvious than expected

Comparing the five typical solopreneur marketing asset scenarios (IG post images, blog covers, logo drafts, product mockups, sales page heroes) against the three tools' official docs and community reviews, their strengths and weaknesses split pretty clearly—not a matter of "which one's better," but "which one's right for what."

**IG posts**—scenarios needing fast output and consistent style. SeaArt generates fast and its free credits can sustain daily posting, but the detail quality generally falls short of Flux in community discussions.

**Blog covers**—where vibe and color harmony matter. Flux clearly leads here. With a scenario like "solo developer working late at night," the depth of lighting layers and emotional weight that Flux delivers is something the other two struggle to match.

**Logo drafts and sales page heroes**—whenever the composition needs English text rendered in the image, that's Ideogram's territory. Flux can generate English too, but it sometimes spells letters wrong. Ideogram's text stability is considered the best among AI image tools from 2024 to 2026 across multiple benchmark reviews [Source: https://https://about.ideogram.ai/2.0].

**Product mockups**—all three are decent, but none beats dedicated mockup tools (like Placeit or Smartmockups). If your marketing assets often need products placed in real scenes, instead of forcing a general-purpose AI image tool, it's better to separately get a dedicated mockup tool.

**Chinese prompt适配度**—SeaArt has the most stable reviews in Mandarin-speaking communities. Flux struggles more with pure Chinese prompts, usually requiring keywords translated to English. Ideogram sits somewhere in between.

## Decision Matrix: Your needs → Which one to pick

Bringing together what we、整理ed into a comparison table:

- **Need: "Massive daily IG posts, just need consistent style"** → SeaArt, free credits are enough
- **Need: "Blog covers need vibe and quality"** → Flux, find a platform offering free credits
- **Need: "Logos, posters, sales page heroes, need English text in the image"** → Ideogram, text rendering is currently the strongest
- **Need: "Mainly Chinese prompts, don't want to learn English prompting"** → SeaArt
- **Need: "Product mockups"** → None of the three are top picks. Recommend getting a separate tool like Placeit or Smartmockups instead

## Three budget combos: Pure Free, Under $10, Under $30

For solopreneurs, this is the real question—how much savings over a year.

**Pure Free combo**: SeaArt (daily IG posts) + Ideogram free tier (small batch of text posters daily) + Flux via Replicate, fal.ai, or Together AI's free credits (occasional blog covers). Yearly cost: $0. Downside: switching between three platforms, workflow gets a bit fragmented.

**Under $10 combo**: Pick one paid option (usually Ideogram Basic—text rendering is a genuine need worth paying for), others stay free. Yearly cost around $100. Compared to Midjourney Basic Plan at ~$96/year [Source: https://docs.midjourney.com/hc/en-us/articles/30246098951821-Comparing-Midjourney-Plans], it's about the same, but keeps the flexibility of switching between multiple tools.

**Under $30 combo**: Ideogram + one Flux platform paid plan. Yearly cost ~$300–360. At this price point it's actually close to Midjourney Standard Plan—whether to go this route depends on how much you value "not being locked into a single tool."

## The fastest path to上手 in 30 minutes

Each tool's path from "sign up" to "first usable image":

**SeaArt**—log in with Google, homepage has a prompt box directly, paste Chinese in and hit generate, see results within minutes.

**Ideogram**—after signing up, choose Free tier. Interface is more intuitive than Midjourney, just type English prompts directly in the input box. When you need text, wrap it in quotes (example: `a poster with "Launch Week" in bold`).

**Flux**—fastest route is Replicate.com, find flux-schnell model, sign-up credits allow dozens of runs. Interface leans toward engineers, but the functionality is straightforward [Source: https://replicate.com/black-forest-labs/flux-schnell].

## 收束

Midjourney going paid is actually an opportunity for solopreneurs to reassess their tool stack. Not everyone needs the strongest image generation tool—most times what you need is "good enough, swappable, not locked in."

Pay for what deserves payment, leave the rest on free tier—this principle is more practical than "finding the cheapest."

Free credit allowances across platforms shift with policy changes; double-check the current offerings before signing up.
