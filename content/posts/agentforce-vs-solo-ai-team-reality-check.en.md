## What Is Agentforce Actually Selling? Turning Enterprise AI Brokers Into SaaS

Salesforce packages Agentforce as an "enterprise-grade AI intelligent agent platform," built around the Atlas Reasoning Engine—that thing that can break down complex requests into bite-sized tasks, evaluate step-by-step, execute autonomously, and scale across teams and departments [Source: https://www.salesforce.com/agentforce/]. The pricing is pure Salesforce style: Flex Credits, conversation-based billing, or per-user licensing—pick one [Source: https://www.salesforce.com/tw/agentforce/pricing/].

Here's an even bigger signal. Gartner predicts that by 2029, autonomous AI will independently resolve 80% of common customer service issues, delivering 30% in operational cost savings [Source: https://www.cxtoday.com/contact-center/agentic-ai-gartner-predicts-80-of-customer-problems-solved-without-human-help-by-2029/].

Nice story. But here's the blind spot Judy AI Lab caught: Agentforce's target customers are IT departments and big enterprises—not solopreneurs running a one-person show.

## What Judy AI Lab's AI Team Looks Like

Judy AI Lab's team runs five Agents. J handles strategy and COO duties, running on Claude Opus; Mimi and Ada handle marketing and product engineering, both on MiniMax M2.7; Lily runs content, hosted on OpenRouter's Hermes model; and Yue does QA, using the Gemini subscription version.

Compared to Agentforce, the biggest difference here is—it's not a platform-provided capability. It's something Judy cobbled together herself. Each Agent has its own role, tools, inbox. Need a new feature? Just tweak the prompt, add a skill—no waiting for some SaaS vendor's roadmap.

Sounds like a hassle, but for small biz owners, this is exactly what Agentforce can't deliver: full customization freedom, no seat limits, run as long as you want.

## Let's Do the Math: Subscription Costs Aren't Even Close

First, let's lay out the official Claude pricing. Pro plan is USD $20/month, Max is $100/month, Team is $30/month per person—so for a 5-person team, that's at least $150/month minimum. Go annual, and it's $25/month per person, so $125/month for 5 people [Source: https://www.anthropic.com/pricing]. MiniMax and Gemini subscription plans also come in under three digits per month.

Add it all up, Judy AI Lab's five-Agent setup runs in the few hundred dollars range—no conversation-based billing traps, no per-seat overhead.

Over on Agentforce, here's the public pricing: Flex Credits at USD $500 per 100k credits, conversation-based pricing at $2 per conversation, and user licensing starting at $125/month per person [Source: https://www.salesforce.com/tw/agentforce/pricing/]. Sounds transparent—but for small biz owners, none of these three paths are friendly. Conversation-based billing means the more you use, the more you pay. Per-seat adds $125 every time you add another person. And Flex Credits require buying in bulk upfront. Bottom line: designed for companies with IT budgets to spare.

## Three Paths for SmallBiz Owners—Pick Your Spot

Path one, full DIY: Use Claude Code or similar Agent CLIs to hook into MCP (Model Context Protocol—think of it as the standard plug for AI talking to external tools), write your own prompts, set up cron jobs. Steepest learning curve, but lowest cost, max flexibility.

Path two, use Claude Pro or ChatGPT Plus as a "semi-auto assistant": No coding involved—just treat AI as your copywriter, report generator, FAQ handler. Twenty bucks a month gets AI into your workflow first, then you decide if you need to level up.

Path three, the Agentforce route: Already have Salesforce CRM, have an IT team ready to integrate, customer service ticket volume high enough to need automatic triage. That's when Agentforce's value actually covers its subscription cost.

No right or wrong paths—just what fits.

## Three Red Flags: Only Then Consider Agentforce

One, your conversation volume grows so big that self-hosted subscriptions can't cover it—and your marginal cost actually goes higher than Agentforce's $2-per-conversation rate.

Two, compliance or audit becomes non-negotiable—certain industries need SOC 2, ISO 27001 certifications, stuff you can't get DIY.

Three, your Agent count goes from 5 to 50, you need role-based access, you need audit logs. That's when the $125-per-month-per-person value outweighs the flexibility loss.

Until those three red flags show up, a few hundred bucks a month to run your whole operation? That's currently the best deal for solopreneurs.

The question was never "Agentforce vs DIY." It's which red flag you're standing on.
