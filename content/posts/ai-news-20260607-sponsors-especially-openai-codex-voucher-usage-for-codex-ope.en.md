## 📰 Key Highlights

> The "Build Small Hackathon" organized by Gradio and Hugging Face has attracted four major sponsors — OpenBMB, OpenAI, NVIDIA, and Modal. The total prize pool exceeds $48,000 in cash and physical prizes, with each participant receiving personal credits including a $100 OpenAI Codex voucher, $250 Modal compute credits, and $20 Hugging Face credits. Registration closed on June 3, 2026, with the development period running from June 5 to June 15 — a full ten days. The theme focuses on "small open models," requiring participants to submit a working demo within the deadline, using efficient lightweight open-weight models to demonstrate practical functionality under limited compute resources. This competition reflects the industry's growing attention to the "small but powerful" model approach — shifting away from simply chasing larger parameter counts toward exploring real deployment feasibility in resource-constrained environments.

## 💬 JudyAI Lab Perspective

Gradio and Hugging Face have teamed up with four major sponsors for the "Build Small Hackathon," with a $48,000 prize pool betting on the "small open model" approach — this is a clear signal that the industry is shifting from the scale race to practical deployment.

The competition setup itself tells you everything — the theme isn't "the strongest model," but "build a working demo with lightweight open models." We've noticed this reflects an emerging shift in evaluation logic: from "big parameters, high benchmark scores" to "can still deploy, can still work under limited compute." The sponsor lineup is also worth noting — $100 in OpenAI Codex credits plus $250 in Modal compute lets individual developers run through an entire development cycle with extremely low barriers. This perfectly validates the core argument for the "small model" approach: lowering the resource barrier so more people can actually get hands-on, not just read papers.

If you're considering whether to try small models, here's a question worth asking yourself: can your application run under constrained compute? That answer determines how likely it is to actually see real-world deployment.
