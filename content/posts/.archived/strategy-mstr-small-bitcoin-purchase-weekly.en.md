## This Week's Headline: Why Did Strategy Hit the Brakes?

In the third week of March 2026, one of the biggest stories in crypto came from the world's largest corporate Bitcoin holder — Strategy (formerly MicroStrategy, ticker: MSTR).

The company founded by Michael Saylor purchased **1,031 BTC** last week at an average price of **$74,326**, totaling approximately **$76.6 million**.

At first glance, $76.6 million is still a significant sum. But compared to the **$1 billion-plus** weekly purchases in the two prior weeks, this "small" buy triggered widespread market discussion.

## Why the Sharp Reduction in Purchase Size?

Strategy's shift from billion-dollar weekly buys to sub-$100 million can be attributed to several factors:

### 1. Strategic Pause in Financing Windows

Strategy funds its Bitcoin purchases primarily through convertible note and preferred stock issuances. After weeks of aggressive fundraising, the market needs digestion time, and the company may be waiting for the next optimal issuance window.

### 2. Caution Amid Falling Prices

BTC is currently trading around $70,000, while Strategy's average cost sits at $75,694. With an approximately 8% paper loss already on the books, slowing the purchase pace can be seen as a risk management measure.

### 3. Geopolitical Uncertainty

Bitcoin held above $70,000 this week largely due to market attention on Iran-US diplomatic talks. During periods of geopolitical ambiguity, even the most committed bulls may choose to wait on the sidelines.

## 762,099 BTC: How Heavy Is the Unrealized Loss?

Strategy's 762,099 Bitcoin, at an average cost of $75,694, represents a total investment of approximately **$57.7 billion**. At BTC's current price of ~$70,000, the portfolio is worth roughly **$53.3 billion** — an unrealized loss of about **$4.4 billion**.

That number looks alarming, but context matters:

**Long-term Strategy vs. Short-term Volatility**: Michael Saylor has repeatedly stated that Strategy's Bitcoin approach is "never sell." For a company treating BTC as a permanent balance sheet asset, short-term drawdowns are acceptable within the strategic framework.

**Accounting Rule Impact**: Under FASB's updated fair value accounting rules for crypto assets, unrealized gains and losses flow directly into earnings reports. This creates quarterly volatility for Strategy's financials but doesn't affect cash flow.

**Market Sentiment Indicator**: As the world's largest corporate BTC holder, every Strategy purchase (or pause) gets amplified by markets. A reduced buying pace doesn't equal bearishness, but it does dampen optimism.

## Geopolitical Undercurrents: How Iran-US Talks Affect Bitcoin

Bitcoin traded in a tight range above $70,000 this week, with market attention largely fixated on diplomatic progress between Iran and the United States.

### Why Geopolitics Matter for Bitcoin

Bitcoin is often called "digital gold" and viewed as a safe-haven asset during geopolitical tensions. But unlike traditional gold, Bitcoin's response to risk-on versus risk-off sentiment is more nuanced:

- **Escalating tensions → Short-term selloff**: Extreme geopolitical risk typically triggers broad liquidation, including crypto
- **Diplomatic progress → Market stabilization**: Positive signals from talks help restore risk appetite
- **Sustained uncertainty → Range-bound trading**: Markets tend to wait for clarity before committing

The current Iran-US talks sit in the third category: "dialogue exists but no clear outcome" — which explains BTC's narrow consolidation around $70,000.

### Trader Perspectives

For active traders, this environment suggests:

- **Short-term**: $70,000 is the critical psychological support level; a break below could trigger cascading stop losses
- **Medium-term**: Wait for geopolitical clarity before establishing significant positions
- **Long-term**: Gradually accumulate during fear — the classic "be greedy when others are fearful" approach

## The Four-Year Cycle Thesis: Is a Q4 Reversal Coming?

Against the backdrop of cautious market sentiment, SkyBridge Capital founder Anthony Scaramucci has offered a different perspective. He has publicly stated that Bitcoin's four-year cycle remains intact, with price appreciation expected in Q4 2026.

### What Is Bitcoin's Four-Year Cycle?

The four-year cycle is closely tied to Bitcoin's halving mechanism:

| Halving Date | Post-Halving Peak | Gain |
|-------------|-------------------|------|
| November 2012 | ~$1,100 (late 2013) | ~100x |
| July 2016 | ~$19,800 (late 2017) | ~30x |
| May 2020 | ~$69,000 (late 2021) | ~8x |
| April 2024 | In progress | TBD |

Each halving cuts Bitcoin's new supply issuance by 50%. With constant or increasing demand, this theoretically drives prices higher. The most recent halving occurred in April 2024, and by historical patterns, 2025-2026 should be the ascending phase of this cycle.

### Scaramucci's Logic

His thesis goes beyond just technical cycle analysis:

1. **Growing institutional adoption**: Strategy, Tesla, BlackRock's BTC ETF, and other institutional players continue to expand their positions
2. **Maturing regulatory frameworks**: Global crypto regulation is shifting from "ban" to "regulate"
3. **Macro cycle tailwinds**: A rate-cutting environment favors risk assets, including Bitcoin

### The Counter-Argument

The four-year cycle thesis also has limitations:

- **Tiny sample size**: Only three complete halving cycles exist — statistically insufficient for strong predictive power
- **Changed market structure**: Institutional participation and derivatives markets are orders of magnitude larger than in 2016; historical patterns may not repeat
- **Macro risks**: Geopolitical events, regulatory shocks, and black swan events could all break the cycle
