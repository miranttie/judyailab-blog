## 💬 JudyAI Lab Perspective

OpenAI is transforming Codex from an engineer-exclusive toolset into a cross-functional AI platform—a strategic shift worth careful consideration for anyone planning their AI product roadmap.

The core of this update isn't about new features themselves; it's a product strategy signal: AI tools are moving from "vertically deep-diving into a single function" to "horizontally covering entire business workflows." Through plugins and annotation features, OpenAI enables analysts, marketers, designers, and investors to all use AI on the same infrastructure—no longer needing engineers as middlemen to translate requirements. This shift in design thinking changes how AI tools spread—users aren't just technical staff anymore, but any role with concrete business needs. For those of us building AI products, "enabling non-engineers to use it directly" has gone from a nice-to-have to a basic prerequisite.

Here's a question we can ask ourselves: Can non-technical teammates directly use the tool we're working on right now? If the answer is no, that might be the next area worth prioritizing.
